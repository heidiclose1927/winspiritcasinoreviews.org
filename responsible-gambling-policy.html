Understanding Responsible Gambling

Responsible gambling means:

  • Gambling for Entertainment: Viewing gambling as a form of entertainment, not a way to make money or recover losses.
  • Setting Limits: Establishing and sticking to personal limits for time and money spent on gambling.
  • Not Chasing Losses: Accepting losses as part of the game and avoiding the temptation to gamble more to win back lost money.
  • Balancing Gambling with Other Activities: Ensuring gambling does not interfere with personal, family, or professional responsibilities.
  • Staying Informed: Understanding the odds and rules of the games you play.

Warning Signs of Problem Gambling

It's important to be aware of the signs that gambling might be becoming a problem. Consider if you or someone you know is:

  • Spending more money or time on gambling than intended.
  • Feeling restless or irritable when trying to cut down or stop gambling.
  • Trying to hide the extent of gambling from family and friends.
  • Gambling to escape from problems or feelings of anxiety, guilt, or depression.
  • Chasing losses or trying to win back money.
  • Borrowing money to gamble or to pay gambling debts.
  • Neglecting work, school, or family responsibilities due to gambling.
  • Experiencing financial difficulties due to gambling.

If you recognize any of these signs in yourself or someone you know, it's crucial to seek help.
